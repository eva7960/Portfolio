Hello I'm Eva!

I’m a senior Computer Science student at the University of Washington (Math minor) with a passion for helping people solve problems through technology. I’m especially drawn to Solution Engineering because it sits at the intersection of technical problem-solving, communication, and real customer impact.

My technical background includes strong proficiency in Java and object-oriented programming, along with full stack development experience using HTML/CSS, JavaScript, and Python. Through coursework and hands-on projects in AI/ML, HCI, Cloud Computing, and Web Design, I’ve built a solid foundation in building user-focused systems, debugging complex issues, and understanding how software components work together end-to-end.

Outside of tech, I bring 5 years of customer-facing experience in a fast-paced, high-volume restaurant environment. I started as a Host and was promoted to Server based on strong performance and customer communication. This role taught me how to understand what customers want, ask the right questions, and translate their needs into clear, accurate outcomes. I’m comfortable working under pressure, handling issues when things go wrong, and staying calm while keeping the customer experience strong. I also regularly communicate across teams — coordinating with other front-of-house staff as well as the kitchen — to solve problems in real time and keep service running smoothly. More recently, I stepped into a Bookkeeping role in addition to serving, creating a training manual and onboarding a new hire to ensure continuity after staffing changes. I’m excited to bring that same customer-first mindset, communication style, and problem-solving approach into a Solution Engineer role in tech.

I also served as President of Women in Computing Sciences, where I led meetings, coordinated across student organizations and faculty, and organized large-scale events including UHackathon 2025 and the UW Tri-Campus Game Jam. When I stepped into the role, I had never run a club before, so I learned everything from scratch—building structure through officer feedback, member input, and continuous iteration on how we operated. Before transitioning out of leadership, I created a detailed onboarding manual for incoming officers to ensure continuity, and I later shared it with another student organization so they could use the same framework as well.

I’m currently seeking opportunities in Product Management, Solutions Consulting, or customer-facing technical roles where I can combine my technical skills, communication strengths, and love for helping others.
